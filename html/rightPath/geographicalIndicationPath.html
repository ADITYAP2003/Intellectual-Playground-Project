Path of Geographical Indications

1. Understand Geographical Indications (GIs)

Geographical indications identify products as originating from a particular region or locality, where a certain quality, reputation, or other characteristic is essentially attributable to its geographical origin.

2. Identify Eligible Products

Identify products that have unique qualities or characteristics derived from their geographical origin and may qualify for geographical indication protection.

3. Research Existing GIs

Conduct research to determine if there are existing geographical indications for similar products in your region or in other jurisdictions.

4. Establish Producer Association

Establish a producer association or group to represent the interests of producers and stakeholders involved in the production of the geographical indication product.

5. Document Product Characteristics

Document the unique qualities, production methods, and other characteristics that define the geographical indication product.

6. Apply for Protection

File an application for geographical indication protection with the relevant intellectual property office or authority, providing evidence of the product's distinctiveness and link to its geographical origin.

7. Examination and Registration

Your application will undergo examination to verify the eligibility and distinctiveness of the geographical indication. If approved, it will be registered.

8. Enforcement of Rights

Enforce your geographical indication rights against unauthorized use by third parties, including imitations or misuses that could mislead consumers.

9. Promotion and Marketing

Promote and market your geographical indication product to raise awareness of its unique qualities and geographical origin among consumers.

10. International Recognition

Seek international recognition and protection for your geographical indication through treaties or agreements that recognize GIs.
